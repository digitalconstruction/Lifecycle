Introduction back to ToC

The goal the Lifecycle Ontology is to represent the product-process relations over construction lifecycles, covering activities, tasks, and sequences, their interrelations to stakeholders involved, the required information, and other resources.

The developed methodological schema results in the six-dimensional intercommunication framework including Activity, Stakeholder, BLCS, LOD, BIM data and Use case.

A major step towards the development of an ontology is the development of a precise understanding of the scope, the content and the relations between the constituent elements of the ontology. The complete methodology is represented in two stems called Product representation and Process representation. The Product representation indicates the relation between BIM objects and their Level Of Detail (LOD) needed in Building Life-Cycle Stages (BLCS) through the involved activities in it.

Similarly, the Process representation describes the role of different stakeholders involved in the renovation interventions or activities. According to the framework definition, the stakeholder roles are categorized dependent on data development, process and consumption activities. All the activities are grouped into different use cases needed for the specific tools or domain tasks involved in the renovation process.
